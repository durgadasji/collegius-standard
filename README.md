# The Collegius Standard

**Version:** v0.1.6
**Status:** Working draft. All six sections present.
**License:** CC BY 4.0 (Regis Chapman)

---

## What this is

The Collegius Standard specifies the structural conditions a self-curating contributor body must meet to function as a credibly neutral, funder-independent distribution mechanism. It defines what a Collegius is (a tensegrity configuration, not an organizational type), what interface it exposes to funders, what internal coordination it must have, what coordination structural conditions apply, and what state it must make legible to an external conformance tool.

**Naming set:** The atomic unit is a Collegius. Its members are Collegenii (singular: Collegenius). The plural of Collegius is Collegii. CaaS (Collegius-as-a-Service) is the service category for implementations.

The name derives from the Latin root shared by "collegial" and "colleague" — a body of practitioners in sustained peer relationship around shared work. That root is already familiar in English; the technical term Collegius gives it structural precision.

---

## The structural problem it addresses

Projects in ecosystem funding are routinely caught between two incompatible pressures: funders want accountability, contributors want sovereignty. Structures that satisfy one typically violate the other. The Collegius Standard resolves this by separating what funders commit to (a domain and a mechanism) from what Collegenii govern (who is in the registry and at what weight). The interface is thin enough to be stable and transparent enough to be accountable. Funders cannot govern membership. Collegenii cannot redirect funds. Both conditions are enforced by the mechanism, not by cultural norms.

---

## Primary empirical reference

[Protocol Guild](https://protocol.guild/) arrived at the Collegius configuration independently, through practical necessity in Ethereum L1 core contributor attribution. The Collegius Standard generalizes that configuration into a specification any ecosystem can adopt. Protocol Guild is the reference implementation, not the template.

---

## Relationship to the Coordination Structural Integrity Suite

The Collegius Standard is downstream from the [Coordination Structural Integrity Suite](https://github.com/coordination-structural-integrity-suite/suite) (CC BY 4.0, Regis Chapman). Section 4 of the standard names compliance requirements for six suite standards as structural conditions of Collegius operation. It is the first specification built directly on top of the suite as structural backbone.

## Open questions

Section 6 of the standard names six open questions: membership criterion design for new domains, transition from existing projects, multiple Collegius membership, Collegius dissolution, the CaaS Viewer specification, and FCT readiness. These are structural design questions, not gaps in the specification's current scope.
