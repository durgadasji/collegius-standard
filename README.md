# The Clan Standard

**Version:** v0.1.5
**Status:** Working draft. All six sections present.
**License:** CC BY 4.0 (Regis Chapman)

---

## What this is

The Clan Standard specifies the structural conditions a self-curated contributor group must meet to function as a credibly neutral, funder-independent distribution mechanism. It defines what a clan is (a tensegrity configuration, not an organizational type), what interface it exposes to funders, what internal governance it must have, what coordination structural conditions apply, and what state it must make legible to an external conformance tool.

The atomic unit is a Clan. CLaaS (Clan-As-A-Service) is the service category a company would build to implement this standard. The standard itself is The Clan Standard.

---

## The structural problem it addresses

Projects in ecosystem funding are routinely caught between two incompatible pressures: funders want accountability, contributors want sovereignty. Structures that satisfy one typically violate the other. The Clan Standard resolves this by separating what funders commit to (a domain and a mechanism) from what contributors govern (who is in the registry and at what weight). The interface is thin enough to be stable and transparent enough to be accountable. Funders cannot govern membership. Contributors cannot redirect funds. Both conditions are enforced by the mechanism, not by cultural norms.

---

## Primary empirical reference

[Protocol Guild](https://protocol.guild/) arrived at the clan configuration independently, through practical necessity in Ethereum L1 core contributor attribution. The Clan Standard generalizes that configuration into a specification any ecosystem can adopt. Protocol Guild is the reference implementation, not the template.

---

## Relationship to the Coordination Structural Integrity Suite

The Clan Standard is downstream from the [Coordination Structural Integrity Suite](https://github.com/coordination-structural-integrity-suite/suite) (CC BY 4.0, Regis Chapman). Section 4 of the standard names compliance requirements for six suite standards as structural conditions of clan operation. It is the first specification built directly on top of the suite as structural backbone.

## Open questions

Section 6 of the standard names six open questions: membership criterion design for new domains, transition from existing projects, multiple clan membership, clan dissolution, the CLaaS Viewer specification, and FCT readiness. These are structural design questions, not gaps in the specification's current scope.
